Gym Tracks

Research Questions:

  • What conclusions can we draw about the social discourse of the pandemic from words used in tweets?
  • How did people react emotionally to various developments such as vaccinations, masks, and COVID developments?
  • How did the discussions about COVID evolve as the pandemic progressed?

Why We Care:

  • To gain a deeper understanding of what topics were important to people during the pandemic
  • To offer insights into how a future pandemic might be better managed

What We Did:

  • Looked at the influence and sentiments of chosen pandemic keywords: Mask, Doctor, Lockdown, Restriction, School, and Freedom
  • And countries: China, UK, Canada, Italy, and India

The Process:

  • Mined one year's worth of tweets (~40 Gigabytes) from the Twitter API
  • Filtered the results based on the keywords mentioned above
  • Performed sentiment analysis using NLTK Natural Language Tool Kit
  • Worked with my team to implement software to analyze the collected data, and visualize the network using Gephi and NetworkX Network Analysis in Python

Results:

  • The social discourse cannot be summed up in one sentence. Through sentiment analysis we can see that there was a general discontent with governments at that time. There were also evolving thought patterns on some of the keywords we focused on - for example: the overall sentiment for vaccine began very negative in January 2020, but ended positively in December 2020.
  • The discussions evolved in multiple ways. It appears as though it was dependent on different aspects of what and who was involved in the pandemic. For example, a general distaste for the Government, as well as certain countries, and a sort of flip-flop of sentiment towards things like Vaccine, Masks, Lockdown and Restriction
